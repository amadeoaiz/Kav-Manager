1. Welcome -- What is Kav?

KavManager is a desktop application for reserve unit commanders. It tracks soldier presence, manages mission tasks, and generates fair duty schedules using a linear programming (LP) solver. It includes a Matrix chat bot that lets soldiers view schedules, swap shifts, manage gear, report issues, and log unplanned tasks.

2. How the Engine Works

The scheduling engine divides time into day and night periods (the boundary defaults to 23:00-07:00, configurable in Settings). Each period is split into uniform time blocks -- typically 60 to 120 minutes long. A linear programming solver then assigns soldiers to blocks, optimizing for three things simultaneously: fair workload distribution, adequate rest spacing, and full task coverage.

Day and night are treated as independent fairness domains -- the solver balances day workload separately from night workload. A soldier's day hours and night hours are tracked independently. The solver tries multiple block lengths (60-180 minutes) and picks the configuration that produces the best overall schedule.

Tasks can be fractionable (split into blocks across multiple soldiers) or fixed (all assigned soldiers stay for the entire duration -- no rotation or block-splitting). Hardship scores (1-5) weight how much each task "costs" a soldier in fairness terms -- a difficulty-5 guard post counts five times more than a difficulty-1 admin task when balancing loads.

How Fairness Works

The solver remembers who worked more than their fair share recently and gives them a break next time. Here is how:

  • Recent history matters most. The solver looks at each soldier's hours over the past 14 days compared to the daily unit average. Recent days count much more than older days -- work from two weeks ago barely registers. No one carries a permanent debt.
  • Excess drives assignment cost. If a soldier worked more than average recently, they become more "expensive" to assign. The solver naturally prefers soldiers who have worked less, evening things out over time.
  • Within each schedule run, the solver also balances total hours across all present soldiers -- it tries to minimize both the average gap from fair share and the worst individual gap.
  • Day and night are independent. Day hours do not affect night fairness and vice versa. A soldier who did many day shifts does not get compensated with fewer night shifts -- each domain is balanced on its own.
  • Returning soldiers start fresh. A soldier who was absent returns with zero recent excess. They are not punished for being away, nor do they have banked credit from before -- everyone is measured by what they actually worked in the recent window.
NOTE: The schedule optimizes over the entire planning window, not just one day. If a soldier appears over-assigned today, it may be because they were idle for the past three days. Check the Stats tab for the full picture.

3. First-Time Setup

Basic Configuration

Open the SETTINGS tab. Under the UNIT sub-tab:

  • Unit name -- your unit's codename or identifier (shown in the title bar).
  • Night window -- the hours that define "night" for scheduling purposes (default: 23:00 start, 07:00 end). Tasks during these hours are balanced separately from daytime tasks.
  • Reserve period start/end -- the dates of your current reserve stint. The app auto-detects boundaries from draft/presence intervals, but you can override them here. This controls which date range the Stats tab and solver consider.

Chain of Command

Also in the UNIT sub-tab, set up to three soldiers as your command chain (primary, secondary, tertiary). The app automatically resolves which commander is "active" at any given time based on presence -- if the primary is absent, it falls through to the secondary, then tertiary.

By default, the active commander is excluded from task assignments. You can override this per-task using the "Include Commander" toggle in the task dialog.

4. Matrix Bot Setup

Why Matrix?

Matrix is an open, decentralized communication protocol with end-to-end encryption enabled by default. Messages between soldiers and the bot are encrypted and cannot be read by third parties. Matrix is not owned by any company, and there is no data harvesting or advertising. The protocol is federated: soldiers can use any Matrix server and still communicate with the bot.

Commander Setup

  1. Create a bot account on a Matrix server (e.g., matrix.org). Go to the server's registration page and create an account that will serve as the bot (e.g., @kavbot:matrix.org).
  2. Download a Matrix client (e.g., Element -- available on desktop and mobile) and log in with the bot account. This is needed to get the access token.
  3. Get the bot's access token. In Element: Settings > Help & About > scroll to "Access Token" (under Advanced). Copy it.
  4. In KavManager, go to SETTINGS > MATRIX CHAT sub-tab. Enter:
    • Homeserver URL -- the server URL (e.g., https://matrix.org)
    • Bot username -- the bot's full Matrix ID (e.g., @kavbot:matrix.org)
    • Access token -- the token copied from Element
  5. Save and restart the app. The bot starts automatically if configured. Check the terminal output for [BOT] Matrix bot starting in background to confirm.

What to Tell Your Soldiers

  1. Each soldier downloads a Matrix client (Element app on phone or desktop).
  2. Each soldier creates a Matrix account (on the same server as the bot, or any federated Matrix server).
  3. Each soldier sends a direct message to the bot account -- the bot will greet them and ask them to select a language (English or Hebrew).
  4. The commander must enter each soldier's Matrix ID (e.g., @name:matrix.org) in their soldier record on the SOLDIERS tab. Without this link, the bot cannot identify them.

What the Bot Can Do

  • All soldiers: view schedule, team schedule, tasks, stats, swap shifts, manage gear, report issues, log unplanned tasks.
  • Privileged users (command chain + Sargent role): commander menu (readiness, stats, create task, create from template, reconcile) and notification settings.

5. Adding Soldiers

Go to the SOLDIERS tab.

Creating a Soldier Record

  • Click Add Soldier and fill in: name, phone number, Matrix ID, and roles.
  • Roles: Every soldier implicitly has the "Soldier" base role -- you never need to assign it. Specialized roles (Driver, Medic, etc.) are addons on top of that. A soldier with the "Driver" role can still be assigned to any task requiring "Soldier".
  • Matrix ID: Enter the soldier's Matrix username (e.g., @yoavi:matrix.org) so the bot can identify them.

6. Readiness, Presence Grid & Leave

The Presence Grid

The GRID tab shows a monthly board with rows for soldiers and columns for days. Each cell displays the soldier's status for that day:

  • Present (green) -- soldier is on base for the full day.
  • Partially present (amber) -- soldier is on base for part of the day (arrival/departure times are recorded).
  • Absent (red) -- soldier is not on base.
  • Drafted, no data (grey) -- soldier is in the reserve rotation but no presence has been recorded for this day yet.
  • Not drafted (blank) -- soldier is not in the reserve rotation.

Right-click a cell to mark a soldier as present, partially present, or absent. You can also select a range of days and apply a status to all of them at once. Right-click a soldier's name to toggle their drafted status or set them as "Active for entire reserve period."

Draft Intervals vs Presence Intervals

  • Draft intervals define the period during which a soldier is drafted for reserve duty. Being drafted does not mean the soldier is present on base -- it means they are part of the active rotation. A soldier can be drafted but absent (on leave, sick, etc.).
  • Presence intervals record when a soldier is actually on base. Only soldiers with presence status "Present" are eligible for task assignments.

Unit Readiness

The HOME tab displays a monthly calendar where each day is color-coded by readiness status:

  • Surplus (bright green) -- more present soldiers than required.
  • OK (medium green) -- present soldiers meet requirements exactly.
  • Partial (amber) -- below requirements but close.
  • Critical (red) -- below minimum, uncovered tasks, or missing required roles.
  • Empty (dim) -- no tasks or requirements set for this day.

Readiness is computed by comparing the number of present soldiers (and their roles) against the combined requirements from active tasks and mission requirements for that day.

Mission Requirements

Mission requirements define the minimum staffing levels for a date range, independent of specific tasks. They are configured on the HOME tab by right-clicking a day on the calendar.

  • Set a label (e.g., "Exercise Alpha"), minimum soldiers, and required roles (e.g., 2x Driver, 1x Medic).
  • Requirements can span multiple days. When multiple requirements overlap on a day, the strictest values are used (maximum min_soldiers, union of required roles).
  • Readiness status combines both mission requirements and active task demands.

Leave and Replacements

When a soldier requests leave, the app can find replacement plans to maintain readiness. On the SOLDIERS tab, select a soldier and use the leave coverage tool.

  • The solver identifies critical days -- days where the unit drops below mission readiness without the requesting soldier.
  • It then searches for replacement candidates among other soldiers, ranked by least disruption (fewest already-planned present days).
  • Single replacement: one soldier covers all critical days.
  • Split replacement: two soldiers split the critical days between them.
  • Applying a solution marks the requesting soldier as absent for the leave period and marks replacements as present on their assigned days.

7. Creating Tasks

Go to the TASKS tab.

Task Fields

  • Name: The task name. You can also select a saved template from the dropdown -- it will auto-fill all fields with smart date defaults.
  • Start / End time: The task window. Times snap to a 15-minute grid (e.g., 08:00, 08:15, 08:30, 08:45).
  • Soldier count: How many soldiers are needed simultaneously.
  • Difficulty (1-5): The hardship score. Higher difficulty means the task "costs" more in fairness calculations -- the solver accounts for this when balancing workloads.
  • Roles: Which roles are required (e.g., 1x Driver, 2x Soldier). Only soldiers with matching roles are eligible.
  • Fractionable: When ON, the task is split into fair time blocks across multiple soldiers (e.g., a 6-hour guard post becomes 4x 90-minute shifts). When OFF, the assigned soldiers cover the entire window with no rotation -- all stay for the full duration.

Eligibility Controls

  • Exclusion list: Specific soldiers who should never be assigned to this task (e.g., for medical or personal reasons).
  • Include Commander: By default OFF -- the active commander is excluded. Toggle ON if the commander should be eligible for this task.

Task Templates

  • Saving: After filling in task fields, click "Save as Template" to store it for reuse. Templates save time-of-day, duration, roles, difficulty, and soldier count.
  • Loading: In the task name field, type to filter saved templates. Selecting one auto-fills all fields. For tasks that cross midnight, the dates default to today/tomorrow.
  • Managing: Go to SETTINGS > SAVED TASKS to view, edit, duplicate, or delete templates.

8. Running the Schedule (Reconcile)

Reconcile runs the LP solver to allocate soldiers to all active tasks, producing a complete schedule.

How to Run It

  • Click [ RECALCULATE SCHEDULE ] in the top bar, or use the reconcile button on the TASKS tab.
  • A progress dialog appears while the solver works (typically under 10 seconds).
  • After completion, all tabs refresh with the new schedule.

UNCOVERED Tasks

If the solver cannot fully staff a task, it marks it as UNCOVERED. This usually means there are not enough present soldiers, or time conflicts make full coverage impossible. When UNCOVERED tasks exist:

  • A warning dialog lists the affected tasks.
  • If any UNCOVERED task has "Include Commander" off, a retry dialog offers to flip it on and re-run -- this adds the commander to the candidate pool, which may resolve the coverage gap.

Pinned Assignments

If you have manually edited any assignments (they become "pinned"), reconcile will ask what to do: keep pinned assignments (solver works around them), clear them (full re-solve), or cancel.

9. Manual Adjustments

After reconcile, you can fine-tune the schedule by hand.

  • Edit a block: Double-click any assignment block (on the HOME tab schedule panel or the TASKS tab assignment table) to open the Block Edit Dialog.
  • Change soldier: Use the CHANGE button to swap the assigned soldier. The picker shows available soldiers sorted by role priority, with hours +/- stats.
  • Add / Remove: Add extra soldiers to a block or remove existing ones.
  • Pinned: Any manually edited assignment becomes "pinned" -- it survives the next reconcile (unless you choose to clear pinned assignments).
  • Swap: Soldiers can also swap assignments with each other through the Matrix bot.

10. Statistics

The STATS tab shows unit-wide workload fairness data.

Controls

  • Period selector: Whole period / Month / Week / Day -- with navigation arrows.
  • Day/Night toggle: Combined view or Day vs Night split.
  • Weighted/Absolute:
    • Weighted (default): hours divided by present days -- gives per-present-day fairness. A soldier present for 3 days who worked 6 hours = 2.0 h/day. Fairer comparison when soldiers have different presence lengths.
    • Absolute: raw total hours, regardless of presence duration.

What the Numbers Mean

  • +/- values show hours above or below the unit average. A soldier at "+1.2h" has worked 1.2 hours more than average; "-0.8h" means 0.8 hours less. Green = below average, orange/red = above average.
  • Fairness spread: the gap between most-loaded and least-loaded soldiers.
  • Charts: "Hours by Soldier" (stacked by difficulty) and "Hours Over Time" (stacked by difficulty per time bucket).

PDF Export

Click [ Export PDF ] to save stats as a PDF. Options: "Current view" (what is on screen) or "Full report" (all combinations of weighted/absolute x combined/day-night).

11. Exports

Google Sheets

The app can export schedule, presence grid, and equipment data to Google Sheets. Setup steps:

  1. Create a Google Service Account: Go to Google Cloud Console, create a project (or use an existing one), enable the Google Sheets API, then go to IAM & Admin > Service Accounts and create a service account. Under the service account's Keys tab, click Add Key > Create new key > JSON. Save the downloaded JSON file somewhere accessible on your computer.
  2. Prepare the target Google Sheet: Create a new Google Sheet (or use an existing one). Click Share, and add the service account email address (found in the JSON file -- it looks like name@project.iam.gserviceaccount.com). Give it Editor access. Copy the sheet's URL or its ID (the long string in the URL between /d/ and /edit).
  3. Configure in KavManager: Go to SETTINGS > EXPORTS. Paste the Google Sheet ID or URL in the spreadsheet field. Set the path to the JSON credentials file you downloaded.

Export buttons appear on the HOME tab (schedule), GRID tab (presence matrix), and RASHMATZ tab (team equipment).

PDF / CSV

  • Default export directory -- set in SETTINGS > EXPORTS. If not set, the app prompts for a save location each time.
  • Stats PDF -- exported from the STATS tab (see Section 10).
  • Grid PDF -- exported from the GRID tab. Shows the monthly presence matrix with status codes (P = present, PA = partial, A = absent).

12. The Matrix Bot -- Commands

The bot is a menu-driven chat interface. Soldiers message it directly and navigate numbered menus.

Soldier Commands (everyone)

  1. My Schedule -- personal day/night schedule with date navigation
  2. Team Schedule -- all soldiers grouped by task
  3. Tasks -- task list with coverage status
  4. Swap Assignment -- pick your assignment, pick a candidate, send request (15-min timeout for the other soldier to accept/decline)
  5. Log Unplanned Task -- multi-step: describe, start, end/ongoing, count, roles, confirm. Ongoing tasks get hourly check-ins.
  6. Report Issue -- creates a request record and notifies privileged users
  7. My Gear / Team Gear -- add, remove, list personal and team gear
  8. My Stats -- weighted/absolute mode, rank, day/night split
  9. Change Language -- English or Hebrew

Privileged User Commands

Who is privileged: command chain members (set in Settings) and soldiers with the Sargent role. Privileged users see two extra menu items:

  • Commander Menu: unit readiness (soldier-by-soldier breakdown), unit stats, create task, create task from template, and trigger reconcile.
  • Notification Settings: toggle two configurable categories:
    • soldier_reports -- issue reports and unplanned task logs
    • gear_changes -- team gear additions/removals
    Defaults: commanders get both ON, sergeants get both OFF.

Always-on notifications (not configurable): schedule changes affecting individual soldiers, and UNCOVERED task alerts after reconcile or detected by the 120-second schedule check loop.

13. Tips & Troubleshooting

Common Issues

"All tasks show UNCOVERED"

Not enough present soldiers for the tasks you have created, or time conflicts make full coverage impossible. Reduce the number of simultaneous tasks, extend their windows, lower soldier count requirements, or mark more soldiers as present.

"Soldier not appearing in assignment options"

Check three things:

  • Presence: Is the soldier marked as present for the task's time window?
  • Roles: Does the soldier have the role required by the task?
  • Exclusion list: Is the soldier on the task's exclusion list or excluded as the active commander?

"Schedule looks unfair"

The solver optimizes over the entire planning window, not just today. A soldier who looks over-assigned today may have been idle for the past several days. Check the Stats tab -- look at the +/- values over the whole reserve period, not just the current day. Also check weighted vs absolute mode.

"Bot not responding"

  • Check that the homeserver URL in Settings matches your Matrix server.
  • Verify the bot access token is correct and has not expired.
  • Check the terminal output for [BOT] log lines -- errors are printed there.

"Reconcile takes too long"

Normal reconcile completes in under 10 seconds. If it takes much longer, check whether you have an unusually large number of overlapping tasks or very long task windows. The solver's problem size scales with tasks x soldiers x blocks.

General Tips

  • Run reconcile after any change to tasks or presence -- the app will prompt you when it detects changes.
  • Use task templates for recurring duties to avoid re-entering the same fields.
  • Check stats regularly to catch fairness drift early.
  • The bot's Commander Menu allows reconciles and task creation from chat.
